Seismic services in El Paso address site response and ground motion hazards shaped by the Rio Grande Rift and deep basin sediments, with assessment and design following ASCE 7‑22 and local amendments. Our approach includes soil liquefaction analysis to evaluate saturated fluvial deposits along the valley floor, and seismic microzonation that maps amplification zones across varying subsurface profiles, providing the geotechnical backbone for code‑compliant, risk‑informed decisions.

These studies support essential infrastructure, healthcare facilities, and commercial developments where performance‑based design is required. For critical structures demanding enhanced resilience, we integrate base isolation seismic design to decouple superstructures from ground motion. Every deliverable ties directly to El Paso’s seismic setting, ensuring practical, constructible solutions from feasibility through final design.
